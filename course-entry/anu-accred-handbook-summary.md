# ANU Academic Accreditation Handbook — Structural Requirements Summary

Source: *ANU Academic Accreditation Handbook, parts 1–5 (Awards, AQF Curriculum, Program Design, Subplan Design, Course Design).*  
Use this as a reference when reviewing courses, programs, majors/minors, or the whole school curriculum.

---

## 1. Programs (Awards)

### Nomenclature
- Titles must use "of" as separator: *Bachelor of Computing*, *Master of Computing*.
- Qualification category and field of study must both be capitalised.
- Parenthetical qualifiers are restricted: `(Honours)`, `(Advanced)`, `(Online)` only.
- Max 79 characters including spaces.
- Names cannot be revised after introduction — changes require disestablishment and a new award.

### Accreditation cycle
- Max 5-year accreditation period; reviewed on expiry.
- Programs not recommended for reaccreditation receive a last-term admit date.
- Early review triggered by: retention < 80%, or majority of courses with SELT satisfaction < 50%.
- Computing programs not professionally accredited must explicitly state this in all published material.

---

### Program structural requirements by award type

#### Bachelor Degree (AQF Level 7) — 144 units
- Max **96 units** of specified discipline/area studies (includes discipline depth ≥ 48 units, identifiable via majors).
- Min **48 units** of electives, expressed as "units from completion of elective courses offered by ANU" (unless prevented by professional accreditation).
- Max **60 units at 1000-level** (unless professional accreditation requires otherwise).
- Min **18 units at 3000-level** (Depth Principle).
- Min **12 units** meeting the TD-GA (Transdisciplinary Graduate Attribute) (Broader Context Principle).
- Up to 12 units of other forms of experiential learning permitted.
- Sub-plans: a sub-plan cannot appear in program orders unless it requires ≥ 12 units of courses not compulsory at program level.
- Minors are elective only; non-language minors cannot be listed in program orders (may be in "further information" section).
- Design principles: Distinctiveness, Developmental (scaffolded), Depth (≥ 18u at 3000-level), Broader Context, Flexibility (24u shareable in Flexible Double Degrees).
- Admission quality floor: Selection Rank 80; exit program: Diploma.

#### Bachelor Degree + 1 Year Immersion (AQF Level 7) — 192 units
- Admission by internal transfer only, after completing 48 units at ANU with GPA ≥ 5.0.
- Must align with standard 144-unit Bachelor requirements plus a 48-unit immersion component.

#### Bachelor Honours Degree — +1 Year (AQF Level 8) — 192 units (144 + 48)
- All Honours-component courses coded at **4000-level**.
- Admission requires weighted average ≥ 70% calculated from best 36 units in the cognate major (excluding 1000-level courses).
- Must include **research training ≥ 12 units** of summative assessment at AQF Level 8.
- Must include **research project ≥ 12 units** of summative assessment at AQF Level 8.
- Final Honours grade uses HONS code; schema: H1, H2A, H2B, H3; calculated as Σ(mark × units) / Σ units.
- Exit program: Bachelor Degree.

#### Bachelor Honours Degree — Embedded (AQF Level 8) — 192 units
- Must align with standard 144-unit Bachelor requirements.
- Honours components coded at **4000-level**.
- Must include **research training ≥ 12 units** and **research project ≥ 12 units** of summative assessment at AQF Level 8.
- Honours grade (H1, H2A, H2B, H3) WAM formula approved by Academic Board; NCN/WN = 0.
- Exit program: Bachelor Degree; admission floor: Selection Rank 80.

#### Bachelor of Philosophy (Honours) / R&D (AQF Level 8) — 192 units
- Selection Rank 98; exit program: Bachelor Degree.
- PhB (3+1 Honours model): Years 1–3 min 18u ASC + 18u ASE/ASC; Year 4 min 24u Honours research project.
- R&D (embedded): Years 1–2 min 24u ASE/ASC; Year 3 min 12u ASC; Year 4 min 12u ASC.

#### Graduate Certificate (AQF Level 8) — 24 units
- Must consist entirely of **6000-, 7000-, and/or 8000-level courses**.
- Exception: 1000/2000/3000-level language courses may be included up to **25% of global requirement**.
- Admission: Bachelor (or equivalent) with GPA ≥ 4.0; or 5 years professional experience (ANZSCO Skill Level 1).
- No exit program.

#### Graduate Diploma (AQF Level 8) — 48 units
- Must consist entirely of **6000-, 7000-, and/or 8000-level courses**.
- Exception: language courses at 1000/2000/3000-level up to **25% of global requirement**.
- Admission: Bachelor or Graduate Certificate with GPA ≥ 4.0.
- Exit program: Graduate Certificate.

#### Masters Degree — Coursework (AQF Level 9) — 48, 72, or 96 units
- Constituted by **6000-, 7000-, and 8000-level courses** only.
  - Exception: language courses at 1000/2000/3000-level up to **25% of global requirement**.
- Must require completion of at least **24 units** (non-Advanced) or **48 units** (Advanced) of courses that are **not co-taught with 1000-, 2000-, or 3000-level courses** (standard teaching events must be separate; guest lectures may be shared).
- Must include **research training ≥ 12 units** of summative assessment at AQF Level 9.
- Must include a substantial research/capstone/scholarship project (≥ 100% of a 6-unit course, or ≥ 50% of two 6-unit courses), demonstrating planning and execution.
- Masters (Advanced): includes thesis of **≥ 24 units**; GPA ≥ 6.0 for admission.
- Sub-plan listing requires ≥ 12 units of courses not compulsory at program level.
- Admission (48u, cognate): Bachelor (GPA ≥ 5.0) or Bachelor + GradCert (GPA ≥ 4.0) or GradDip (GPA ≥ 4.0) or 10 years professional experience.
- Admission (72/96u): Bachelor (GPA ≥ 5.0), or Bachelor Hons (GPA ≥ 4.0), etc.

#### Masters Degree — Extended (AQF Level 9) — 144 or 192 units
- Constituted by **6000-, 7000-, and 8000-level courses**, plus up to **24 units of 4000-level courses only**.
- Exception: language courses at 1000/2000/3000-level up to **25% of global requirement**.
- Same research/capstone and co-teaching requirements as coursework Masters above.
- Includes JD (legal practice) and MChD (medical practice) — title "Doctor of …" is permitted.
- Admission: GPA ≥ 5.0 (Masters programs); MChD as approved by Academic Board.

#### Masters Degree — Research (AQF Level 9) — 96 units
- Constituted of **8000- and 8900R-level courses** only; lower-level enrolment requires Delegated Authority approval.
- Research component must be **≥ two-thirds** of the qualification.
- Must include a thesis of up to **60,000 words**.
- Must be titled *Master of Philosophy* [with augmentation] unless an exception is approved by Academic Board.

#### Doctoral Degree (AQF Level 10) — 96–192 units
- Constituted of **9000- and 8000-level courses**; enrolment in 6000/7000-level requires Delegated Authority approval.
- **Max one-third** coursework and/or skills training.
- Must include a thesis of up to **100,000 words** (unless thesis by compilation or creative works).
- Admission: Bachelor Honours or Masters with thesis ≥ 24 units, or Master of Philosophy.

---

### Double degrees

| Type | Minimum total length |
|---|---|
| Double Bachelor | Longest component + 48 units |
| Double Masters (coursework) | Longest component + 24 units, or ≥ 96 units (whichever is larger); no component < 48 units |

- Core requirements of *both* awards must be met in full.
- Max 1000-level courses per component in Double Bachelor: 48 units.
- Double counting allowed but does not reduce total unit value — substitute courses taken from free electives.

---

### Program orders (legislative instrument rules)
- Self-contained: must not reference external materials, other programs, or require students to seek further advice.
- Must not contain compulsory sub-plans — if sub-plans are listed, there must be a choice between ≥ 2.
- Unit values expressed as exact numbers or min/max — never as "number of courses" or "semesters".
- Level references use 1000–9000 numeric codes, not "first year", "second year" etc.
- Order of requirements: Total Unit Value → Global Requirements (by level or alpha code) → Detailed Requirements.
- Electives expressed as: `"[minimum of] XX units from completion of elective courses offered by ANU"`.
- Max/min global levels use "may" (max) and "must" (min/exact).
- Sub-plans in orders: `"[48/24] units from completion of one of the following [majors/minors/specialisations]: …"`.

## 2. Sub-plans: Majors, Minors, Specialisations

### Majors

#### AQF Level 5 (Diploma concurrent) and AQF Level 7 (Bachelor) — 48 units
- Courses: **1000-, 2000-, and 3000-level only**.
- Max **18 units at 1000-level**.
- Min **18 units at 3000-level** (more is permitted).
- Min **24 units (≥ 4 courses) unique from other majors** (distinctiveness requirement).
- Self-contained except where the major is restricted to programs where prerequisites outside the major are compulsory at program level.
- No additional admission requirements (except the Music Performance Major).
- Incompatibility: must be listed as incompatible with any corresponding minor (both cannot appear on transcript).

#### AQF Level 8 (Bachelor embedded Honours) — 48 units
- Courses: **1000-, 2000-, 3000-, and 4000-level only**.
- Max **18 units at 1000-level**.
- Min **18 units at 3000- or 4000-level**.
- Min **24 units (≥ 4 courses) unique from other majors** (distinctiveness).
- Self-contained (except where restricted to specific programs with compulsory pre/co-reqs).

**Key rule for all majors:** A specific major cannot be compulsory in a plan — if required, its courses must be written out individually in the program orders.

---

### Minors — 24 units (AQF 5–9)

- Courses: **1000-, 2000-, 3000-, and 4000-level only**.
- Self-contained (except where restricted to programs with compulsory pre/co-reqs outside the minor).
- No additional admission requirements.
- **Must be incompatible with any corresponding major** — both cannot appear on a transcript/AHEGS simultaneously.
- Language minors only: may appear in program orders; all other minors may not.
- Cannot be included in programs ≤ 24 units total.

---

### Advanced Minors — 24 units (AQF 5–9)

- Courses: **2000- and 3000-level only** (plus 4000-level for embedded Honours programs).
- Must correspond to an identified major; has the **same learning outcomes** as that major.
- Must have **at least the same requirements for 3000-level** (or 4000-level for embedded Honours) as the corresponding major.
- **No 1000-level courses** — students must take a placement test/proficiency assessment that results in exemption from all 1000-level major courses.
- Self-contained: completable in exactly 24 units including all pre/co-reqs.
- Named: *"Advanced [X] Minor"* where [X] exactly matches the corresponding major name.
- Incompatible with the corresponding major (both cannot appear on transcript/AHEGS).
- Language advanced minors only: may appear in program orders.

---

### Specialisations

#### AQF Level 7 Bachelor — 24 units
- Courses: **2000- and 3000-level only**.
- Min **12 units at 3000-level**; orders must include global requirement `"a minimum of [X, at least 12] units of 3000-level courses"`.
- Must have an identified co-requisite major (or ≥ 48 units of compulsory program courses not overlapping with the specialisation).
- Major + specialisation combination must be self-contained.
- Min 18 units of courses not compulsory at program level (to be listable in program orders).

#### AQF Level 8 Bachelor +1 Honours — 48 units
- Courses: **4000-level only**.
- Includes both research training and research project/thesis.
- Must be self-contained.
- Has formal admission requirements approved by Academic Board.
- Thesis cannot be double-counted across two specialisations.

#### AQF Level 8 Bachelor Embedded Honours — 24 units
- Courses: **2000-, 3000-, and 4000-level**.
- Min **12 units at 4000-level**; orders must include `"a minimum of [X, at least 12] units of 4000-level courses"`.
- Self-contained unless restricted to programs where pre/co-reqs are compulsory at program level.
- No additional admission requirements.

#### AQF Level 9 Masters (coursework) — 24 units
- Courses: **6000/7000-level (co-taught/not co-taught) and 8000-level**.
- Min **12 units at 8000-level**; orders must include `"a minimum of [X, at least 12] units of 8000-level courses"`.
- Self-contained unless restricted to programs with compulsory pre/co-reqs.
- No additional admission requirements.

**Key rule for all specialisations:** A specific specialisation cannot be compulsory in a plan.  
**Sub-plan listing threshold:** A sub-plan cannot appear in program orders or on a transcript unless it requires ≥ 12 units not compulsory at program level (applies to all sub-plan types).

## 3. Courses

### Required published information (every course)
All must be endorsed by the College Education Committee, approved by the Delegated Authority, and published on Programs and Courses:
- **Course overview**
- **Learning outcomes**
- **Assessment**: each item — individual vs group, which LO(s) assessed, weighting, whether hurdle
- **Workload**: hours per workload type
- **Inherent requirements** (if any)
- **Requisites and incompatibilities** (if any)
- **Assumed knowledge** (if any)

---

### Course level definitions and requirements (Table 17)

#### 1000-level — Introductory undergraduate
- Broad overview of discipline or foundational skills.
- May assume prior high school curriculum knowledge if stated.
- **Pre/co-requisites must also be 1000-level.**
- **Co-teaching**: only with other 1000-level courses. Exception: courses designated as Honours Pathway Courses (HPCs, indicated in description or by suffix "H") may be co-taught with 1000- and 2000-level courses.

#### 2000-level — Intermediate undergraduate
- Explicitly builds on prior tertiary study.
- Prerequisite: ≥ 1 named 1000- or 2000-level course, OR ≥ 24 units of tertiary courses, OR ≥ 12 units in the field/discipline.
- **Co-teaching**: only with 2000-level and/or 6000-level courses. Exception: HPCs (suffix "H" or designated in description) may be co-taught with 2000-, 3000-, and/or 6000-level courses.

#### 3000-level — Advanced undergraduate
- Explicitly builds on prior *advanced* tertiary study.
- Prerequisite: ≥ 1 named 2000- or 3000-level course, OR ≥ 24 units in the field/discipline, OR ≥ 72 units of tertiary courses.
- **Co-teaching**: only with 3000-level and/or 6000-level courses.

#### 4000-level — Honours
- **Reserved exclusively for Honours components** (in +1 or embedded Honours programs).
- Non-Honours courses must not be coded at 4000-level.
- **Co-teaching**:
  - Courses requiring a completed pass degree (direct-entry +1 Honours): only with 4000-, 8000-, 8900R-, and 9000-level courses.
  - Courses in embedded Honours that do not require a completed pass degree: only with 4000-level, and EITHER with 8000/9000-level; OR with 6000-level (if those 6000-level courses are not additionally co-taught with 1000/2000/3000-level); OR with 2000/3000-level courses in a different discipline (at 2-digit FoR level) if the 4000-level courses have demonstrably higher-level LOs and assessment, and neither course is co-taught with 6000/7000/8000-level.
  - **4000-level courses must not be co-taught with 1000-, 2000-, or 3000-level courses** (except the narrow embedded Honours exception above).

#### 5000-level — Microcredentials only
- Reserved for Microcredentials and Microcredential stacks (courses coded at this level pre-October 2019 are grandfathered).
- Must not be used for AQF award courses.
- **Co-teaching**: only with 7000- and 8000-level courses (including where 8000-level is co-taught with 4000-level). Must not be co-taught with 1000-, 2000-, or 3000-level courses.

#### 6000-level — Introductory graduate (co-taught with undergrads)
- Builds on generic knowledge from an undergraduate pass degree; introductory/overview of discipline.
- **A graduate-level course that shares any teaching events with 1000-, 2000-, and/or 3000-level courses must be coded at 6000-level.** Courses not sharing events with those levels must not be coded at 6000-level — they must be 7000-level or higher.
- Pre/co-requisites: 6000- or 7000-level only.
- **Co-teaching**: only with 6000-, 2000-, and 3000-level courses; may also be co-taught with 4000-level if the 6000-level course is not additionally co-taught with 1000-, 2000-, or 3000-level courses.

#### 7000-level — Introductory graduate (not co-taught with undergrads)
- Builds on generic undergraduate pass degree knowledge; introductory/overview.
- Pre/co-requisites: 6000- or 7000-level only.
- **Co-teaching**: only with other 7000-level courses.

#### 8000-level — Advanced graduate
- Builds on cognate knowledge from: prior cognate degree or equivalent work experience; OR ≥ 24 units of 6000/7000-level cognate courses; OR ≥ 96 units towards an ANU vertical double degree (meeting GPA and admission requirements for the Masters component).
- Alternatively, may build on undergraduate pass degree *plus* stated foundational concepts needed for advanced discipline entry.
- Must contribute to AQF Level 9 outcomes (applying advanced body of knowledge for professional practice or scholarship).
- **Co-teaching**: only with 8000-, 4000-, 8900R-, and 9000-level courses. 8000-level courses of < 6 units must not be co-taught except with 9000-level courses.

#### 8900R-level — MPhil courses
- Restricted to students enrolled in a Master of Philosophy.
- **Co-teaching**: only with 8900-, 4000-, 8000-, and 9000-level courses.

#### 9000-level — PhD courses
- Restricted to students enrolled in a Doctor of Philosophy.
- **Co-teaching**: only with 9000-, 4000-, 8000-, and 8900-level courses.

### Workload and units
- Standard unit value: **6 units** = 130 hours average student workload.
- 48 units = 1 EFTSL.
- Non-6-unit courses (multiples of 6) permitted for: internships, fieldwork, international study tours, research projects, theses, clinical requirements.
- 3-unit courses: only permitted at 8000-level (not co-taught with 4000-level) and in Medical School / HDR.
- Zero-unit courses: only where activity is a hurdle to future coursework or professional practice (e.g. WEI).

### Course codes (alpha codes)
- University-wide; must refer to a discipline, not an organisational unit (School, College, Centre, Institute).
- Multi-discipline codes: every course using them must demonstrate integration and include integration-testing assessment.
- Program-specific alpha codes: courses locked to that program; if program disestablished, course must be disestablished or recoded.
- New alpha codes require AQAC approval.
- Credit codes begin "Credit:"; exchange codes begin "Exchange:".

### Teaching currency
- Each course must be taught at least **once in every 3-year period**, or face managed disestablishment.
- Not taught in **6 years** → automatic disestablishment.

### Special topics and repeat enrolment
- Topic-based courses: topic must be assigned before enrolment opens; same topic cannot be reused without CEC endorsement (within 5 years).
- Students cannot repeat a successfully completed course/topic combination towards an award.
- One special-topics course per alpha code per numeric level per organisational unit.

### Course review cycle
| Review type | When required | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| Comprehensive | Every 5 years; mandatory if SELT bottom 5% in College; mandatory if previous review was Streamlined | Design, LOs, assessment, SELT data, grade distribution, LMS, may include peer review |
| Streamlined | Every other 5-year cycle (if previous was Comprehensive) | Constructive alignment, compliance with HESF/AQF, SELT data at College discretion |
| External accreditation | When professional body accreditation covers the course | Satisfies comprehensive or streamlined if CEC confirms; compliance checklist always required |

## 4. Quality Indicators Relevant to Reviews

| Indicator | Threshold / trigger |
|---|---|
| Student retention | < 80% → early program review |
| SELT overall satisfaction | Majority of program's courses < 50% agreement → early program review |
| SELT bottom 5% in College | → course must use comprehensive review (not streamlined) |
| Course not taught | Any 3-year period → managed disestablishment process |
| Course not taught | Any 6-year period → automatic disestablishment |
| Program accreditation lapsed | → last-term admit date applied |
| Sub-plan review | At least every 5 years |
| Admissions standards review | At least every 5 years |

## 5. Professional Accreditation Note for Computing

Computing programs and sub-plans must either:
1. Be professionally accredited, OR
2. Explicitly state in Programs and Courses and all marketing material that the university has not sought or does not hold professional accreditation, with a reference to accredited programs offered by ANU where possible.

Applies to: Actuarial Studies, Accounting, Business Information Systems, Finance, **Computing**, Engineering, Law, Medicine, Psychology, Statistics.
