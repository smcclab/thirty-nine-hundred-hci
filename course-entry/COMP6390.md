## Description

This course will provide an overview of the field of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and will introduce students to key interaction and experience design paradigms, approaches and methods. The course will equip students with theory, methods, and techniques to investigate different design situations, develop and prototype novel design ideas, and engage with the ethical dimensions of technology design.

## Learning Outcomes

1. Explain and illustrate key aspects of human-computer interaction, including relevant theories, ethical considerations, and perspectives.
2. Apply a design and prototyping process to develop interactive systems that address identified user needs.
3. Investigate activities, practices, and contexts of technology use through research methods.
4. Critically evaluate novel technology designs using human-computer interaction approaches.
5. Critically reflect on a designer’s own presence and influence within a technology design and research process.
