## Balance Flags

- **Week 3 (Prototype): ~144 min** — Two involved activities (live coding + sketch demo) total 40 min of activity; slide count is also high. Consider whether the live-coding prototype is always finished in time or routinely runs over.
- **Week 6 (Thematic Analysis): ~132 min** 🟡 — Borderline. The Miro board clustering activity is 20 min and there are 5 activities total. Watch in practice; trimming one pulse check wouldn't help much but shortening the clustering session would.

### Pacing — light

- **Week 5 (Data Analysis): ~59 min** — Only 27 slides and a single questions slide. This is probably notebook/demo-heavy material not captured in the markdown, but as written it looks like half a lecture. Worth adding a slide or two of framing, or explicit signposting that demos run outside the slide deck.
- **Week 10 (Statistical Analysis): ~59 min with 11 section dividers** — Same pattern: 11 section-title slides for only 24 content slides strongly suggests this is demo/notebook-driven. If so, note it explicitly so the lean slide count isn't mistaken for thin content.
- **Week 11 (Expressive Interfaces): ~58 min** — Only 24 slides and 9 sections. Lightest lecture by slide count after weeks 5/10.

### Engagement

- **Week 10 (Statistical): 0 activities** — No engagement breaks in what is likely an already dense numerical topic. Add at least one pulse check or a "predict the outcome" moment before a demo.
- **Week 12 (Human-Centred AI): 0 activities** — 47 slides with no engagement breaks; the estimated 104 min of content slides with no pauses risks trailing off. Add at least one discussion moment in the final lecture.
- **Week 5 (Data Analysis):** Only a "Questions" slide — minimal interaction. Given the topic (interpretivist vs positivist stances) is conceptually rich, at least one getting-answers activity would help.
- **Week 11 (Expressive Interfaces):** Only a "Questions" slide — same concern.

### Content density

- **Week 7 (Interfaces): 110 words/content slide, 5,606 total words** — Densest lecture by word count by a wide margin. Heavy reading overhead for students; consider adding more visual slides or breaking long text bullets.
- **Week 11 (Expressive Interfaces): 1.52 images/content slide** 🔴 — Well above the 1.0 norm. With only 23 content slides crammed with 35 images, this is visually saturated. Some slides likely have side-by-side or stacked images; verify they render cleanly at lecture scale.
- **Weeks 4, 6, 12: ~92–94 words/content slide** 🟡 — Borderline dense; watchable but worth trimming long bullet lists.

### Workshops

- **Week 1 (Making): no explicit task times** — Every other workshop states times for each task. Adding rough timings (e.g., "20 mins") helps tutors pace and flags when the session is at risk of running over with a slow group.
- **Weeks 11–12: no workshops** — Two weeks at the end of semester have lectures only. This is likely deliberate (students working on final projects), but worth confirming the Week 12 "drop-in consultation" slot is clearly signposted to students as the substitute for a workshop.

## Overall Verdict

The course is **front-heavy and back-light**. Weeks 2–3 are overestimated by 40–50 minutes — the Design and Prototype lectures are the two most likely to run over in practice and are also the weeks students are newest to the material. Weeks 10–12 swing to the opposite extreme: slide counts drop sharply, activities disappear, and the last two weeks have no workshops. The middle of the course (Weeks 4–9) is well-balanced, with consistent workshop structure (~90 min, 3–5 timed tasks) and good lecture engagement (2–5 activities each). The main interventions needed are: **trim weeks 2–3** (remove one involved activity each or reduce content slides by ~10), **add minimal engagement to weeks 10 and 12**, and **add timing annotations to workshop 01**.
